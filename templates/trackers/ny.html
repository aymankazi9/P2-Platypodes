Hotspot | New York

by: Pragadeesh Raj

The Issue

Ever since the beginning of this pandemic, which has been farther than most of our memories, New York was the first to hit 1 million cases. 1 million cases may not seem like much today because we have currently have states that have surpassed 3 million cases and are approaching 4 million cases. However, back then in the situation of the pandemic, this was a high number in comparison to the overall national cases. From that point, New York was put under severe lockdown protocols and the situation looked to be positive again. However, now that the protocols are a bit more lenient, the cases have begun to see a rise. But is this purely due to coincidence? What is the definitive reason that New York has so many cases?


These are the questions and phenomena experts such as epidemiologists have been looking to answer and here's what they found. The main issue comes from the location and the inevitable nature of a state with a gateway city; New York City. Without heavy enforcements on lockdown protocols, international travel is not heavily regulated, therefore leading to movement to and from other countries (most commonly Europe). The second reason however, is saddening for many families who have lost loved ones. As case rates go up, it is more and more evident that the American healthcare system was not built to operate efficiently in a pandemic situation. The rise in case counts leads to greater admissions into hospitals. Once a hospital's carrying capacity is reached, however, there's not a lot that can be done so the death rate exponentiates. In a populous area such as New York City, this is a daily problem hospitals face.

Statistics

As more people do their part to stabilize this pandemic, the overall trend is improving. Although still high, New York's average trends for new cases and confirmed deaths are decreasing. More data analysis can and trend analysis are discussed here. Look below for a quick data overview, but check out the tracker page for live updates and more details.

    Current Statistics:
  • Approximately 17 million total cases.
  • Approximately 48,000 total deaths.
  • Approximately 40 million people tested.
  • Approximately 3.7 million people with at least the first vaccine dose.
  • Approximately 1.9 million people with a complete vaccine dose.

What can You Do?

In a difficult time as this, many think there isn't much an average person can do, but that would be very wrong! You don't need to contribute cutting edge research in order to help the situation, in fact, the best help one can offer during this pandemic is to follow safety guidelines. No matter where you are, or how that area is doing in case count, it's always smart to mask up and only leave the house if necessary. Make sure to stay socially distant when engaging in social activities. These are just some of the many important steps you can take.
